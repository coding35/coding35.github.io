Functions should take arguments as input and return a computed value. Functions should not have side effects.

Procedures may or may not take arguments as input and have side effects. Procedures should not return a computed value.

Methods used in OOP are functions or procedures that are associated with a class or object. Methods may or may not take arguments as input and may or may not return a computed value. Methods can have side effects.
