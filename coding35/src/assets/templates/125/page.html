The Manchester Baby computer, also known as the Small-Scale Experimental Machine (SSEM), was the first stored-program computer. It was built at the University of Manchester in 1948. The computer was designed by Frederic C. Williams, Tom Kilburn, and Geoff Tootill. The Manchester Baby computer was a small-scale experimental computer that demonstrated the feasibility of the stored-program concept. The computer was used to test the principles of the stored-program concept and to develop the software for the Manchester Mark 1 computer.
